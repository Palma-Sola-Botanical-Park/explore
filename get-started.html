Step by step

Make Your Observation

"We're not scientists — we're enthusiasts, volunteers, and community members who care about this place. iNaturalist gives us access to a global network of experts who can confirm or correct our identifications. If you think we've misidentified something, bring the debate to the project. That's exactly how it's supposed to work."

— Palma Sola Botanical Park

Every signed plant in the park has a QR code. You scanned it to read about the plant — your phone's already in your hand. So why not take thirty seconds and log what you see for us?

  1. Tap the + button

    Open iNaturalist and tap the green + button.

  2. Take your photos

    Tap the shutter as many times as you want — whole plant, leaves, flowers, fruit, bark. More is better.

  3. Tap the green checkmark

    When you're done shooting, tap the green checkmark to finish adding photos.

  4. Add an identification

    Accept iNaturalist's suggestion or search the species yourself — best guess is fine, the community will help. One exception: if the plant has one of our signs and it says something different, go with the sign.

  5. Verify location, then Upload

    Confirm the address sounds roughly right, then tap Upload — not Save. Save just keeps it on your phone; Upload sends it to the world.

Field craft

Tips for Great Observations

Here's the quiet magic: our scripts read every photo for what the plant is doing — leaf, bud, flower, fruit — so over time the park learns exactly when each specimen does what. You just take the photo. The record builds itself, bit by bit, across everyone who walks through.

None of this is required — but a little care makes your photo far easier for the community to identify.

  • Center your subject — keep the plant in the middle, with no other plants crowding the shot.
  • Capture what identifies it — the whole plant, plus close-ups of leaves, flowers, buds, fruit, and bark.
  • Show scale — a finger or hand next to a leaf or berry gives a size reference.
  • GPS on — make sure location services are enabled before you shoot.
Level up

Now Be an Identifier

So far, so easy — anyone can point a phone and shoot. But if you want to really learn your craft and start down the road to becoming a naturalist, this is the good part. Identifying is where snapshots turn into real botany: you confirm what you're sure of, puzzle through what you're not, and your eye gets sharper every single time. That's the reward — real skill, earned one observation at a time.

It's easiest on a computer — a big screen and a real keyboard make it fly — but you can do it right from the app too. Here's how to go deeper:

  1. Join the Palma Sola project

    Go to our project page at inaturalist.org/projects/palma-sola-botanical-park. If you're signed in on the iNaturalist website, you'll see a Join button near the top — click it. Once you've joined, you can see every observation made in the park in one place, not just your own — including which ones still need an ID. That's exactly how we keep track of what's left to confirm.

  2. Help confirm identifications

    Open the Identify tool, look at the photos, and agree with — or gently question — the IDs people proposed. Each confirmation nudges an observation toward a settled identification, and you'll pick up a startling amount of botany doing it.

  3. Read the project journal

    The project's journal and blog is where the deeper conversation lives: what's newly in bloom, which IDs are contested, what we're trying to document next.

This is the scientific level — and honestly, the most rewarding part. There's a real satisfaction in settling an ID and watching it become part of the park's permanent record. Come be a detective with us — we'd love to have you.
