Find Us

Getting here

We're tucked into the northwest corner of Bradenton, in good company — between Robinson Preserve and De Soto National Memorial, just off Palma Sola Bay. Free parking on-site. Paved and natural paths, suitable for most visitors.

♿

Accessibility

Main paths are paved or packed gravel. Some garden areas have natural ground cover. Wheelchairs and strollers can navigate most of the park. Call ahead if you have specific questions.

🐾

Pets Welcome

Well-behaved pets on leash are welcome — please clean up after them. A botanical park is full of plants your dog won't find at home, so keep an eye on what they sniff and nibble; a few can upset a curious pet's stomach. Help us keep the plantings and wildlife undisturbed, and everyone has a good walk.

Hidden Garden Nursery

Plants grown right here

Tucked near the west entrance, our volunteer-run nursery carries plants propagated directly from the collection. Reasonable prices. 100% of sales benefit the park.

Looking for something specific? Call ahead. Volunteers know exactly what's rooted and ready — including Florida natives you won't find at a box store.
