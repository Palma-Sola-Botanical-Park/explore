Pagoda Flower
Clerodendrum paniculatum Lamiaceae
🌍 Non-Native ⚠️ Watch List ✅ Edible & Safe
⚡Quick Hits
  • The flowers are orange-red, but the structure that makes this plant striking is the pagoda-like tiered arrangement of the flower clusters — each cluster stacked above the last in diminishing rings, like a Buddhist temple tower.
  • A member of the mint family (Lamiaceae), confirmed by square stems. Related to the park's Starburst Bush and Turk's Turban.
  • Spreads aggressively by underground suckers. A single plant expands into a colony quickly. Worth knowing before siting it.
  • Hummingbirds and butterflies work the flowers actively. A useful addition to a pollinator planting.
🌍Origin

Non-native. Native to Cambodia, Laos, Thailand, and Southeast Asia. Member of Lamiaceae — the mint family.

🔍More Information
  • The Pagoda Flower earns its name from its flower head, not its individual blooms: dozens of small orange-red tubular flowers are stacked in tiered, pyramid-shaped clusters that rise above the big, dark, broadly lobed leaves like the roofs of a pagoda. The display sits at the branch tips and can be a foot or more tall.
  • The tiered structure suits pollinators that hover and probe - hummingbirds and butterflies work the tubular flowers, and the plant is a reliable nectar draw through the warm months. It belongs to the genus Clerodendrum, in the mint family (Lamiaceae), alongside the park's Starburst Bush and Turk's Turban.
  • It spreads enthusiastically by underground suckers, which is its main drawback - left unchecked it forms a thicket, so it is best given room or contained with a barrier.
🦋Wildlife Value

Hummingbirds and butterflies are frequent visitors. The tiered flower structure presents accessible nectar at multiple heights. The long bloom season and dense flower production make it a reliable pollinator plant.

🔬Reproduction & Identification
Flowers

Orange-red, tubular, in tiered pagoda-like whorls on upright stalks. The stacked arrangement is the defining visual feature.

Leaves

Opposite, broadly ovate, medium green with slightly wavy margins.

Form

Upright shrub, 3 to 6 feet. Spreads by underground suckers to form colonies. Square stems throughout.

What to Look For

The tiered, pagoda-like flower arrangement is distinctive — stacked rings of bloom on an upright stalk. Square stems confirm the mint family.

📝Notes

The Pagoda Flower recorded at Palma Sola grew on Welcome Island and was lost in the storms. The park's Tree Crinums have since expanded into that space, as they tend to do. All park observations predate the loss.
