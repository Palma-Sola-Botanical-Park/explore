How we know what's here

We're not scientists.
Neither are most of our observers.

iNaturalist is our field notebook.

We rely on iNaturalist for final species identifications. We're not scientists — we're enthusiasts, volunteers, and community members who care about this place. iNaturalist connects us to a global network of experts who can confirm or correct our identifications. When we're not sure what something is, we post it and let the community weigh in.

The project is called Palma Sola Botanical Park Collection Project. Every single observation in it was put there by a person walking through this park with their phone. If you think we've misidentified something — bring the debate to the project. That's exactly how it's supposed to work.

Why it matters

  • Every observation is a permanent, timestamped, geolocated scientific record — yours forever
  • Bloom logs build a seasonal picture over years that no single scientist could create alone
  • Wildlife observations reveal which animals use which plants — connections nobody else is tracking
  • The data belongs to everyone. Researchers, educators, future visitors. You made that.

What to photograph right now

  • 🌸 Anything in bloom — log it and it enters the permanent seasonal record
  • 🦜 Any bird you can photograph — even blurry counts, seriously
  • 🦎 Lizards, frogs, and snakes on or near the path
  • 🦋 Butterflies on specific plants — the plant-insect connection is real science
  • 🍄 Fungi — almost nothing documented yet. This gap is yours to fill.
